Streak camera hardware alignment
  1. At all time limit pixel intensity to max. 1000 cts
  2. Blank or turn off the e-beam
  3. Close the shutter and the slit of the streak camera, then move the streak camera backwards
  4. Put the streak camera in alignment mode
  5. Open the shutter and carefully open the slit until the signal is visible in live view
  6. Adjust the rotation of the slit to illuminate the complete photocathode
  7. Adjust the focus of the input optics to maximize the pixel intensity
  8. Close the slit and move the streak camera back against the SPARC
  9. Turn on the calibration light and close the slit of the spectrograph (Manual focus button). Open the slit of the streak camera to get the signal in live view. Maximize the intensity by moving the focus of the spectrograph manually. Turn the calibration light off (Manual focus button)
  10. Turn on the e-beam and find the CL spot by adjusting the height of the streak camera with the lab-jack
  11. Adjust the spectrograph detector offset by adjusting the offset value based on the horizontal position of the CL-spot on the readout cam (change offset using shrkconfig commands in terminal). The spot should be centered on the horizontal axis (wavelenght axis)
  12. Select the magnification corresponding to the input optics of the streak camera in the streak tab


 Trigger delay calibration
  1. Check that the e-beam settings fit the sample
  2. Load an existing calibration file for a specified e-beam voltage or perform a new trigger delay calibration
  3. Turn on the e-beam blanker
  4. Choose the largest time range setting
  5. Switch on the streak mode (checkbox)
  6. Optimize the MCP-gain and the exposure time until the signal (a line) is sufficient
  7. Choose a smaller time range setting if multiple lines are visible
  8. Move the line to the center by adjusting the trigger delay
  9. Perform the same procedure for all higher zoom levels
  10. Save the calibration file specifying the e-beam voltage
